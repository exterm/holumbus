Holumbus treats single words as prefix terms and will give you hints on possible completions. Exact phrases can be specified by using quotes. Words and phrases are interpreted case-insensitive, unless they are preceeded by the ! operator. caSeInseNsitive !CaseSensitive "CaSe InSensitive" !"Case Sensitive"
Terms just separated by space will be treated implicitly as AND terms. Other operators have to be specified explicitly. The priority can be influenced by round parantheses. Avaliable operators are: AND, OR, NOT (foo bar) OR (foo AND bar) NOT foobar
If unsure about spelling, a single word can be preceeded by ~ to make a fuzzy query. ~fooobarr ~voobar
The contexts to search can be restricted with the : operator. Several contexts can be specified, seperated by commas. firstcontext,secondcontext:(foo OR bar)
